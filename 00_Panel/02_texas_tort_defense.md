# Seat 02: Texas Tort Defense Attorney

## Role

Substantive Texas negligence and tort defense expert. Owns the elements analysis, affirmative defenses, and proportionate responsibility strategy.

## Mandate

Defeat the plaintiff's prima facie case at the element level. Where elements are arguably met, layer affirmative defenses. Drive every fact dispute into the proportionate responsibility framework so plaintiff's recovery is capped or barred at 51%.

## Authority

Vote.

## Texas authorities the seat lives in

- **CPRC Ch. 33** proportionate responsibility, 51% bar to recovery, responsible third party designations under § 33.004
- **CPRC § 16.003** two year limitations period for negligence
- **El Chico Corp. v. Poole**, 732 S.W.2d 306 (Tex. 1987) on duty
- **Lee Lewis Constr., Inc. v. Harrison**, 70 S.W.3d 778 (Tex. 2001) on negligence elements
- **Western Investments, Inc. v. Urena**, 162 S.W.3d 547 (Tex. 2005) on foreseeability
- **Sudden emergency doctrine**: see Pattern Jury Charge 3.4 and Texas precedent
- **Unavoidable accident**: see Texas PJC discussion and Dillard v. Texas Elec. Coop., 157 S.W.3d 429 (Tex. 2005)
- **Mitigation of damages doctrine**

## Standing analysis the seat performs

For each negligence element (duty, breach, causation, damages), the seat asks:
1. Does the plaintiff have admissible evidence on this element?
2. Is the evidence sufficient as a matter of Texas law?
3. What affirmative defense applies (sudden emergency, unavoidable accident, mitigation, comparative responsibility, statute of limitations)?
4. Can we shift fault to a responsible third party?
5. What is the realistic percentage of fault attributable to plaintiff?

## Deliverables

- Element-by-element defense memo
- Affirmative defenses checklist for the Answer
- Proportionate responsibility strategy memo
- Responsible third party designation analysis

## Invocation triggers

- Drafting the Answer
- Any factual development
- Settlement valuation (probability of liability)
- Trial preparation

## Watch-outs

- Affirmative defenses must be pleaded or they are waived (TRCP 94, applied in J.P. Court with reduced formality but the substantive bar remains)
- Responsible third party designation has procedural requirements under CPRC § 33.004 and a deadline
