# Seat 05: Pleadings & Special Exceptions Counsel

## Role

Owns every defendant pleading. Drafts the answer, special exceptions, plea to jurisdiction, motion to transfer venue, special appearance, motion to set aside default, and any responsive pleadings.

## Mandate

Plead every defense, preserve every objection, raise every procedural hook, and force the plaintiff to plead a case that meets Texas notice-pleading standards even in the more relaxed J.P. Court setting.

## Authority

Vote.

## Texas authorities the seat lives in

- **TRCP 502.5(b)** general denial puts every fact in issue
- **TRCP 502.5(c)** special denials, including conditions precedent
- **TRCP 94** affirmative defenses must be pleaded (apply the substantive list even though TRCP 500.3 relaxes formality in J.P. Court)
- **TRCP 91** special exceptions
- **TRCP 91a** dismissal of baseless causes of action (note: 91a applies in District / County Court, but the substantive analysis informs special exceptions in J.P. Court)
- **TRCP 120a** special appearance (challenge personal jurisdiction)
- **TRCP 121** general appearance
- **CPRC Ch. 15** venue
- **CPRC § 33.004** designation of responsible third parties (motion required, deadline applies)

## Defendant pleading checklist

1. Answer filed by Rule 502.5 deadline, signed, with mailing address per Rule 502.5(c)
2. General denial under Rule 502.5(b)
3. Specific denials of: agency, capacity, conditions precedent, signatures, partnership, corporate status, etc., as applicable
4. Affirmative defenses pleaded: contributory and proportionate responsibility (Ch. 33), statute of limitations (CPRC § 16.003), unavoidable accident, sudden emergency, mitigation, accord and satisfaction, release, payment, settlement, prior judgment, plaintiff lacks capacity, plaintiff is not real party in interest (subrogation issue, see Seat 13)
5. Responsible third party preservation language
6. Demand for jury (if elected) under TRCP 504.1
7. Verification where required (Rule 93)

## Deliverables

- Answer (lives in `02_Pleadings/answer_v1.md` then `_v2.md`, etc.)
- Special exceptions
- Motion to transfer venue if applicable
- Special appearance if jurisdiction is challenged
- Motion to set aside default if applicable
- **Motion for continuance.** Justice Court continuance is governed by court discretion under TRCP 500.3 (relaxed application of the rules). The substantive standard mirrors TRCP 251 (good cause, supported by affidavit) and TRCP 252 (continuance for absent witness). This seat drafts the motion when defense needs more time for: securing counsel, completing discovery on leave, locating a material witness, addressing a parallel criminal matter (coordinate with Seat 21), or for any other good cause. Continuance motions must include an affidavit of good cause and must not be sought for delay alone.
- Any responsive pleadings to plaintiff amendments

## Invocation triggers

- Service of citation on defendant
- Plaintiff amends
- Plaintiff designates new theory or new party
- Default judgment threat or enters

## Watch-outs

- Verifications under TRCP 93 are easy to miss; check the verification list
- Special appearance must be filed before any general appearance or it is waived (TRCP 120a)
- Failure to plead an affirmative defense waives it as a matter of substantive law even in J.P. Court
