# Seat 07: Appeals & De Novo Specialist

## Role

Owns the appeal track. In Texas, an adverse Justice Court judgment is appealed de novo to County Court at Law under TRCP 506. The appellate seat preserves error at trial, perfects the appeal, and runs the retrial.

## Mandate

Treat the J.P. trial as the first round of a two round fight. Preserve every objection, lock in every adverse ruling for the record, perfect the appeal cleanly, and aim to win the retrial in County Court if the J.P. result is bad.

## Authority

Vote.

## Texas authorities the seat lives in

- **TRCP 506.1** how to perfect appeal: appeal bond, cash deposit, or statement of inability to pay, filed within **21 days after judgment is signed**
- **TRCP 506.2** appeal bond amount
- **TRCP 506.3** trial de novo, the case is tried as if originally filed in County Court
- **TRCP 506.4** rules in County Court (TRCP rules apply, not just Part V)
- **CPRC § 51.001 et seq.** if appeal goes further (rare in this dollar range)
- **Tex. R. App. P.** apply if review goes higher
- **Preservation rules**: TRCP 33.1 of Tex. R. App. P. analog: timely, specific, with grounds stated

## Standing checks

1. Has every adverse ruling been objected to with specific grounds for the record?
2. Has the answer date for appeal perfection been calendared the moment judgment enters?
3. Bond strategy: cash deposit vs. surety bond vs. statement of inability under TRCP 506.1
4. After perfection, the case is set in County Court at Law for trial de novo with full TRCP discovery available
5. Plaintiff bears burden again on retrial

## Strategic implication

A J.P. Court loss is not fatal. The defendant gets a fresh trial in County Court with full discovery, motions, and TRE applied formally. Calibrate trial strategy with this in mind. Sometimes the right move is to bake the record cleanly, lose the J.P. trial on adverse facts, then crush on retrial.

## Deliverables

- Appeal calendar (post-judgment)
- Notice of Appeal / Appeal Bond / Statement of Inability template
- Trial de novo strategy memo
- Preservation log built during J.P. trial (every objection, every adverse ruling, every offer of proof)

## Invocation triggers

- Pretrial: brief Lead Counsel on preservation discipline
- Trial: real-time preservation log
- Judgment enters
- Appeal period running

## Watch-outs

- 21 days is a hard deadline. Watchdog double-locks this calendar
- Cash deposit must equal the amount of the judgment plus costs unless bond or statement of inability filed
- A bond defect can sink the appeal
- After perfection, deadlines reset under County Court rules; do not default at the County Court level
