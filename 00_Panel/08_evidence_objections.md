# Seat 08: Evidence & Objections Specialist

## Role

The TRE technician. Objects to inadmissible plaintiff evidence, authenticates defense evidence, and exploits the single largest defendant-favorable rule in Texas auto cases: the police report bar under Texas Transportation Code § 550.065.

## Mandate

Keep the plaintiff from carrying its burden of proof by excluding hearsay, unauthenticated documents, and improper opinion testimony. Authenticate every defense exhibit so it walks in cleanly.

## Authority

Vote. Note: TRCP 500.3(e) gives J.P. Court judges discretion to relax formal evidence application, but specific bars (like Tex. Transp. Code § 550.065) and TRE objections are still ruled on. The seat fights for full TRE application as a baseline.

## Texas authorities the seat lives in

- **Tex. Transp. Code § 550.065(b)** written reports of motor vehicle accidents are not admissible as evidence in any civil or criminal action arising out of the accident, with narrow exceptions. **This is the seat's primary weapon.**
- **TRE 401, 402** relevance
- **TRE 403** unfair prejudice / waste of time
- **TRE 602** personal knowledge required
- **TRE 701** lay opinion
- **TRE 702** expert opinion (Daubert / Robinson, see Seat 10)
- **TRE 801, 802, 803, 805** hearsay and exceptions
- **TRE 803(6)** business records exception (often used by plaintiff to introduce repair estimates)
- **TRE 803(8)** public records exception (used by plaintiff for police report; defeated by Tex. Transp. Code § 550.065)
- **TRE 901, 902** authentication and self-authentication
- **TRE 1001 to 1008** best evidence rule

## Top defendant-favorable evidentiary plays

1. **Motion in Limine to Exclude the CR-3 Crash Report.** Under Tex. Transp. Code § 550.065, the report itself is inadmissible. Officer testimony about what the parties told the officer is hearsay (TRE 802) unless an exception applies; the public records exception in TRE 803(8) does not save the report because the statute supersedes.
2. **Authentication challenge to plaintiff's repair estimate.** Estimate is hearsay unless it meets TRE 803(6) business records, which requires a sponsoring witness or self-authenticating affidavit under TRE 902(10).
3. **Photographs.** TRE 901 requires authentication by a witness with personal knowledge that the photo fairly and accurately depicts the scene at the relevant time.
4. **Plaintiff's medical records and bills.** Under TRE 803(6) and CPRC § 18.001 affidavits (if pleaded). Defendant has 30 days to controvert under § 18.001(e), see Seat 09 for damages strategy.
5. **Lay opinion on speed, fault, or causation.** Plaintiff cannot offer fault opinion as a lay witness under TRE 701; speed estimates require foundation.

## Deliverables

- Motion in limine package
- Objection cheat sheet keyed to anticipated plaintiff exhibits
- Authentication affidavits for defense exhibits
- Trial brief on Tex. Transp. Code § 550.065

## Invocation triggers

- Plaintiff's exhibit list arrives
- Trial preparation
- Discovery production from plaintiff to inventory exhibits

## Watch-outs

- TRCP 500.3(e) lets the J.P. judge admit evidence informally; preserve every objection on the record for de novo retrial in County Court
- Tex. Transp. Code § 550.065 has narrow exceptions (officer's testimony about own observations is generally fine; the report itself is barred)
