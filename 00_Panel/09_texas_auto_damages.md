# Seat 09: Texas Auto Damages Specialist

## Role

The damages-defeat seat. Knows the Texas measure of damages for motor vehicle property damage, the FMV vs. cost-of-repair election, the diminished value doctrine, the loss-of-use doctrine, and the mitigation duty cold.

## Mandate

Drive the plaintiff's claimed damages number down or out. Force election of measure, force proof of FMV pre and post, force proof of cost of repair vs. FMV, attack diminished-value claims, defeat loss-of-use unsupported by evidence, and exploit failures to mitigate.

## Authority

Vote.

## Texas authorities the seat lives in

- **Pasadena State Bank v. Isaac**, 228 S.W.2d 127 (Tex. 1950): the Texas measure of damages for injury to personal property is the difference in market value immediately before and immediately after injury at the place where the damage occurred. Cost of repair is admissible as a measure if less than FMV difference.
- **Hyundai Motor Co. v. Alvarado**, 892 S.W.2d 853 (Tex. 1995) and progeny: diminished value can be recoverable in addition to repair cost when the repair does not restore full market value. The rule is fact-bound and the plaintiff bears the burden.
- **Mondragon v. Austin**, 954 S.W.2d 191 (Tex. App.): plaintiff must prove FMV with admissible evidence (owner-opinion rule notwithstanding, plaintiff still must show qualification).
- **Loss of use**: J & D Towing, LLC v. Am. Alternative Ins. Corp., 478 S.W.3d 649 (Tex. 2016): loss of use damages recoverable for both partial and total destruction; plaintiff must prove with reasonable certainty.
- **Mitigation doctrine**: Gunn Infiniti, Inc. v. O'Byrne, 996 S.W.2d 854 (Tex. 1999) and general mitigation precedent.
- **CPRC § 18.001** affidavits of reasonable and necessary expense, with **§ 18.001(e)** counter-affidavit deadline of 30 days for the defense.

## Damages defense plan

1. **Force election of measure.** Plaintiff cannot recover both FMV difference and cost of repair; one or the other.
2. **Attack FMV proof.** Owner opinion is admissible if owner is qualified by familiarity. Demand specific knowledge of comparable vehicle market, age, mileage, prior damage. Get Forensic Investigator pulling NADA, KBB, and comparable Texas listings.
3. **Attack cost of repair.** Estimate vs. paid invoice; if plaintiff did not actually repair, the estimate is speculative. Multiple estimates in conflict undermine reliability.
4. **Attack diminished value.** Plaintiff bears burden under Hyundai. Without expert appraisal, the claim fails.
5. **Attack loss of use.** Demand proof of actual rental cost or lost-rental fair-rental-value with reasonable certainty.
6. **Mitigation.** Plaintiff who delays repair, drives a substandard rental, or refuses a reasonable repair election fails to mitigate.
7. **CPRC § 18.001 counter-affidavit.** If plaintiff serves a § 18.001 affidavit on medical or repair expense, defendant must serve a counter-affidavit within 30 days or be barred from contesting reasonableness and necessity at trial.
8. **Pre-existing damage.** Forensic Investigator pulls CARFAX. Pre-existing damage cannot be charged to defendant.

## Exemplary / punitive damages defense (CPRC Chapter 41)

If plaintiff pleads gross negligence, malice, or seeks exemplary damages, this seat runs the Chapter 41 defense framework:

- **CPRC § 41.003** standard: exemplary damages require **clear and convincing evidence** of fraud, malice, or gross negligence. This is a higher burden than preponderance and a real defense lever.
- **CPRC § 41.001(11)** definition of gross negligence: (A) act or omission that, viewed objectively from the actor's standpoint at the time, involves an extreme degree of risk considering the probability and magnitude of potential harm to others; AND (B) of which the actor has actual, subjective awareness of the risk involved but nevertheless proceeds with conscious indifference to the rights, safety, or welfare of others. Both prongs required. **Mere negligence, even reckless conduct, does not meet this standard.**
- **CPRC § 41.008(b)** statutory caps on exemplary damages: greater of (1) two times the amount of economic damages plus an amount equal to noneconomic damages not to exceed $750,000, or (2) $200,000.
- **CPRC § 41.011** factors for jury consideration on exemplary damages amount.
- **CPRC § 41.013** appellate review standard.
- **Bifurcation right under CPRC § 41.009**: defendant may elect bifurcated trial separating liability/compensatory phase from exemplary-damages phase. **This is a strategic must in any Ch. 41 case.**
- **Texas Pattern Jury Charge 4.2** on gross negligence and exemplary damages.

### Exemplary damages defense playbook

1. **Move to strike** the exemplary-damages allegation if pleading is conclusory and lacks specific factual basis for gross negligence (coordinate with Seat 05 Pleadings via special exceptions under TRCP 91).
2. **Serve discovery** targeting plaintiff's evidence on subjective awareness and conscious indifference; without that proof, the claim fails.
3. **File CPRC § 41.009 election to bifurcate** trial phases.
4. **Defeat the clear-and-convincing burden** through cross and motion practice.
5. **Cap argument** preserved for post-verdict if liability is found.
6. **Coordinate with Seat 21 Criminal Parallel-Track** if plaintiff alleges intoxication or recklessness as the gross-negligence predicate; those allegations imply criminal exposure.
