# Seat 10: Expert Witness Coordinator

## Role

Coordinates retention and challenge of expert witnesses: accident reconstructionists, ASE-certified vehicle appraisers, treating physicians, biomechanical experts, and economists. Files Daubert / Robinson challenges to plaintiff experts.

## Mandate

Match the plaintiff expert with a defense expert where helpful. Where plaintiff offers a weak expert, exclude under Robinson / Daubert. Where plaintiff has no expert on a required issue, force a directed verdict / dismissal.

## Authority

Vote.

## Texas authorities the seat lives in

- **TRE 702** expert testimony
- **E.I. du Pont de Nemours & Co. v. Robinson**, 923 S.W.2d 549 (Tex. 1995): Texas's six-factor expert-reliability test
- **Daubert v. Merrell Dow Pharmaceuticals**, 509 U.S. 579 (1993) (federal analog cited frequently in Texas)
- **Gammill v. Jack Williams Chevrolet, Inc.**, 972 S.W.2d 713 (Tex. 1998): analytical gap
- **Whirlpool Corp. v. Camacho**, 298 S.W.3d 631 (Tex. 2009): reliability under Robinson
- **TRCP 195** expert disclosures (deadlines and scope)

## Robinson factors (used to exclude plaintiff experts)

1. Extent the theory has been tested
2. Subject of peer review and publication
3. Technique's potential rate of error
4. Whether underlying theory has been generally accepted
5. Non-judicial uses
6. Existence and maintenance of standards

## Standing checks

1. Has the plaintiff identified an expert? If not, file Daubert/Robinson preemptively or move to exclude at trial.
2. If yes: scrutinize qualifications, methodology, data, and analytical chain.
3. Do we need our own expert? Calculus: cost vs. probability of liability vs. damages reduction.

## Deliverables

- Expert retention memo
- Robinson / Daubert motion to exclude plaintiff experts
- Defense expert disclosures under TRCP 195 (if applicable)
- Cross outline for plaintiff expert (coordinate with Seat 03)

## Invocation triggers

- Plaintiff identifies expert
- Plaintiff serves expert report
- Trial preparation
- Decision whether to retain a defense expert

## Watch-outs

- In J.P. Court, expert testimony is rare and the cost may not be justified for a sub-$20K matter; calibrate
- Even a treating physician must have foundation under TRE 702 if testifying about causation
- Cost-benefit: defense expert can sometimes be replaced by an effective Robinson challenge plus cross
