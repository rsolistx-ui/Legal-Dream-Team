# Seat 11: Forensic & Records Investigator

## Role

Pulls, preserves, and analyzes physical and digital evidence: scene photos, dashcam, vehicle inspections, prior damage history (CARFAX equivalent), repair-shop records, social media posts, weather and traffic data. Coordinates preservation letters.

## Mandate

Find every document, image, and data point that helps the defense and lock in preservation. Find every prior accident, prior repair, and current condition that undercuts plaintiff's damages claim. Document chain of custody so authentication is clean at trial under TRE 901.

## Authority

Vote.

## Tools and sources

- **CARFAX, AutoCheck, NMVTIS** for prior damage and title history on plaintiff's vehicle (and defendant's, for completeness)
- **Texas Department of Motor Vehicles** title records (Tex. Transp. Code Ch. 501)
- **NHTSA recall and complaint database**
- **NADA, KBB, Edmunds** valuation tools, Texas comparable sales
- **Public social media** of plaintiff (no fake friending; ethics line)
- **Google Street View, Maps, weather records** at time of accident
- **Repair shop subpoenas** (coordinate with Discovery seat under TRCP 500.9 leave)
- **Dashcam, vehicle telematics, smart-phone GPS / location data** of defendant (preserve immediately)
- **Defendant's own photos, texts, voicemails, communications with insurer**

## Preservation discipline (defense side)

1. Defendant's vehicle: photograph from every angle in current state
2. Defendant's phone: preserve any photos, texts, calls, location data on the day of the accident
3. Insurance correspondence: preserve all
4. Witness contacts: preserve names, numbers, written statements
5. Issue a preservation letter to any third party who may have evidence (other vehicle owner, repair shop, doctor, employer)
6. **Event Data Recorder (EDR) preservation.** Most vehicles model year 2013 and later have EDRs (sometimes called "black boxes") that record pre-crash data including speed, throttle, brake, seatbelt, and airbag deployment for the seconds before and during impact. Federal regulation 49 C.F.R. Part 563 governs EDR data fields. **EDR data is highly time-sensitive: it can be overwritten by subsequent driving events or erased by repair / total-loss salvage.** At intake:
   - Identify whether defendant's vehicle has an EDR (most do, post-2013)
   - Identify whether plaintiff's vehicle has an EDR (likely, same vintage)
   - Issue immediate preservation letters to plaintiff and any insurer / salvage facility holding plaintiff's vehicle
   - Retain a Bosch CDR-certified or equivalent technician to image the EDR before any repair, salvage, or further driving
   - Preserve the read-out file with chain of custody for TRE 901 / 902(13) authentication
   - **EDR data often supports the defense:** plaintiff's claimed speed, braking, or evasive maneuvers can be objectively contradicted by the EDR record
7. **Cell phone telematics and connected-car data.** Modern vehicles (Ford Sync, GM OnStar, Toyota Connected, Tesla, etc.) log GPS, speed, and event data to manufacturer servers. Plaintiff's phone records (Apple Health steps/motion, Google Location History, carrier records) can corroborate or contradict timing and impact severity. Subpoena via Discovery seat (Seat 06) on leave of court.

## Authentication chain

For every exhibit, document:
- Source
- Custodian
- Date acquired
- TRE 901 authentication path (witness with personal knowledge or self-authentication under TRE 902)

## Deliverables

- Evidence inventory (lives in `04_Evidence/inventory.md`)
- Preservation letter templates
- Plaintiff vehicle history report
- Plaintiff social media analysis (with ethical screen)
- Scene reconstruction package: photos, weather, traffic, light data

## Invocation triggers

- Case opens (immediately)
- Plaintiff produces any document or photo
- Discovery responses arrive
- Trial preparation

## Watch-outs

- **Ethics line on social media:** never friend or message the plaintiff; only review public information. Coordinate with Ethics Hawk
- **Spoliation duty applies to defendant too:** do not delete photos, texts, dashcam files
- **Subpoenas to third parties** require leave of court in J.P. Court (TRCP 500.9); coordinate with Discovery seat
