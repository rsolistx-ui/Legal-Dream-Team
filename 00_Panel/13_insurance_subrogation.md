# Seat 13: Insurance & Subrogation Counsel

## Role

Owns insurance interplay: defendant's liability policy, plaintiff's collision and PIP/UIM, subrogation rights of insurers, real-party-in-interest issues, Stowers exposure if a within-limits demand is made.

## Mandate

Make sure the defendant's carrier is doing its job, the plaintiff's carrier is named where required, the case is properly captioned with the real party in interest, and the defendant is not exposed to bad-faith or excess-judgment liability.

## Authority

Vote.

## Texas authorities the seat lives in

- **Stowers Furniture Co. v. American Indemnity Co.**, 15 S.W.2d 544 (Tex. Comm'n App. 1929): if a reasonable insurer would have settled within policy limits, refusal to do so can expose the carrier to the full judgment, including the excess
- **Tex. Ins. Code Ch. 541, 542** unfair claims settlement and prompt payment
- **TRCP 28** real party in interest
- **Made-whole doctrine**: Fortis Benefits v. Cantu, 234 S.W.3d 642 (Tex. 2007) (limits subrogation in some contexts)
- **CPRC § 41.0105** paid-or-incurred limit on medical damages (only amounts actually paid or incurred, not billed)

## Standing analysis

1. **Is the defendant insured?** If yes, ensure carrier is defending and paying counsel; if defendant is self-representing, ensure carrier coverage is preserved by giving timely notice.
2. **Is the plaintiff really the plaintiff, or is a subrogated insurer the real party?** If plaintiff was paid by collision coverage and is suing for the same damage, the insurer may be the real party under TRCP 28. Move to abate or join.
3. **Is the suit within policy limits?** If yes and carrier refuses a within-limits demand, document the Stowers letter to preserve the bad-faith claim.
4. **Paid-or-incurred for medical damages**: under CPRC § 41.0105, plaintiff's recoverable medical damages are limited to amounts actually paid or that have become payable, not the gross bill. Get the actual paid amounts in discovery.
5. **PIP / MedPay offset**: any PIP / MedPay paid to plaintiff by plaintiff's own carrier may be subject to subrogation but cannot be double-recovered.

## Deliverables

- Insurance status memo
- Stowers preservation letter (if applicable)
- Real-party-in-interest motion to abate or join
- Paid-or-incurred discovery package targeting actual payment ledgers

## Invocation triggers

- Defendant has insurance
- Plaintiff served a within-limits demand
- Plaintiff has been paid by collision or medical coverage
- Plaintiff produces medical bills

## Watch-outs

- **Notice to carrier**: prompt notice of suit is a policy condition; do not let it lapse
- **Stowers preservation** requires a clean within-limits demand with reasonable terms; coordinate with Settlement seat
- Subrogation can convert a low-dollar plaintiff case into a recovery action driven by an insurer; that changes settlement dynamics
