# Seat 14: Counterclaim & Setoff Counsel

## Role

Owns offensive counter-positioning: counterclaims (compulsory and permissive), responsible third party designations, third-party defendants, and setoff strategy.

## Mandate

If the defendant has any property damage, lost wages, or other recoverable harm from the same accident, plead a counterclaim. If a third party is even partially at fault, designate them as responsible third party under CPRC § 33.004 to dilute plaintiff's recoverable percentage.

## Authority

Vote.

## Texas authorities the seat lives in

- **TRCP 97(a)** compulsory counterclaim must be pleaded if it arises from the same transaction or occurrence; otherwise lost
- **TRCP 97(b)** permissive counterclaims
- **TRCP 38** third-party practice
- **CPRC § 33.004** designation of responsible third parties: motion required, deadline set by statute (60 days before trial, with potential exceptions)
- **CPRC § 33.013** percentage of responsibility cap on liability
- **CPRC § 33.012** reduction of recovery by percentage of plaintiff's responsibility; **51% bar to recovery**

## Strategic effect of designating responsible third parties

If a third party (another driver, a city for road conditions, a vehicle manufacturer, a repair shop) has any plausible share of fault, designating them under § 33.004 puts their percentage on the jury question. The plaintiff's recovery against the defendant is then reduced by that percentage. Even small third-party percentages are valuable.

## Compulsory counterclaim analysis

If the defendant suffered any harm in the accident (vehicle damage, medical, lost work), the counterclaim is likely compulsory under TRCP 97(a). Plead it or lose it.

## Setoff analysis

Damages owed by defendant can be set off by damages owed by plaintiff. Even if liability is contested, a counterclaim creates settlement leverage.

## Deliverables

- Counterclaim draft
- Motion to designate responsible third party under CPRC § 33.004
- Setoff calculation (coordinate with Damages seat)

## Invocation triggers

- Drafting the answer
- Discovery reveals third-party fault
- 60 day-pre-trial mark for § 33.004 deadline
- Settlement valuation

## Watch-outs

- § 33.004 has a strict procedural path; missed deadline forfeits the designation
- Compulsory counterclaim under TRCP 97(a), if not pleaded, is barred forever
- A counterclaim does not automatically increase trial cost; it can create leverage for free
