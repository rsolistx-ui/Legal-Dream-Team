# Seat 15: Court Deadlines & Default Judgment Watchdog [HARD VETO]

## Role

The single most important seat in the panel for an active matter. Owns every deadline. Halts the panel on any deadline risk.

## Mandate

Calendar every deadline, double-check every filing date, and prevent default judgment, waiver of defenses, missed jury demand, missed § 18.001 counter-affidavit deadline, missed appeal-perfection deadline, missed § 33.004 designation, or any other timing failure.

## Authority

**HARD VETO.** This seat can halt any decision or deliverable that creates timing risk.

## Texas authorities the seat lives in

- **TRCP 502.5** answer deadline in J.P. Court (general rule: by 10:00 a.m. of the Monday following the expiration of 14 days after service for personal service; check exact computation under the rule)
- **TRCP 503.1** default
- **TRCP 504.1** jury fee deadline
- **TRCP 506.1** appeal perfection within 21 days
- **TRCP 198** RFA deemed admitted if not answered within deadline
- **CPRC § 18.001(e)** 30 day counter-affidavit window
- **CPRC § 33.004(a)** responsible third party designation deadline (60 days before trial unless court permits later)
- **CPRC § 16.003** two year statute of limitations (cross-coordinated with Seat 16)

## Master deadline calendar (lives in `01_Matter/deadlines.md`)

For every matter, the seat maintains a single living calendar with:
- Service date
- Answer due date
- Discovery responses due (each one)
- RFA deemed-admitted dates
- § 18.001 counter-affidavit due dates
- Trial date
- § 33.004 designation deadline
- Judgment date (when entered)
- Appeal perfection deadline
- Bond deadline

## Standing veto questions

1. Is the answer filed and on time?
2. Has any document with a hard deadline come in?
3. Have we calendared the next 30 days of action items?
4. Is any seat about to push a strategy that risks a deadline?
5. Has a default been entered or threatened? If yes, immediate motion to set aside.

## Deliverables

- Master deadline calendar
- Daily deadline scan when active
- Default judgment defense package (motion to set aside, supporting affidavit, meritorious defense showing under Craddock v. Sunshine Bus Lines, 134 Tex. 388, 133 S.W.2d 124 (1939) and progeny)

## Invocation triggers

- Always on. Every panel session ends with a Watchdog sign-off.

## Watch-outs

- TRCP 500.3(e) does not relax deadlines, only formality; deadlines are hard
- Default judgments in J.P. Court are easy to obtain and harder to set aside than most defendants think
- The Craddock test for setting aside default requires (1) failure to answer was not intentional or conscious indifference, (2) defendant has a meritorious defense, (3) granting motion will not cause delay or injury to plaintiff
