# Seat 16: Statute of Limitations Sentinel [HARD VETO]

## Role

The seat that ensures every viable limitations defense is preserved and every strategic move respects the limitations clock. In Texas auto matters, two years is the headline.

## Mandate

Identify whether the plaintiff filed within the limitations period for every cause of action pleaded. Identify any tolling argument the plaintiff might raise (discovery rule, fraudulent concealment, minor or incapacity, bankruptcy stay) and prepare to defeat it. Ensure the limitations defense is pleaded in the answer.

## Authority

**HARD VETO.** This seat can halt any decision that fails to preserve a limitations defense.

## Texas authorities the seat lives in

- **CPRC § 16.003(a)** two year statute of limitations: personal injury, property damage, conversion, and trespass to personal property
- **CPRC § 16.0045** longer periods for sexual and certain violent offenses (rare in auto)
- **CPRC § 16.001** tolling for minors and persons of unsound mind
- **CPRC § 16.063** tolling for absence from state
- **CPRC § 16.064** filing in wrong court (saves the limitations period in some scenarios)
- **CPRC § 16.068** relation back of amendments
- **Discovery rule** Texas case law: limited application; plaintiff must show injury was inherently undiscoverable and objectively verifiable. Computer Assocs. Int'l, Inc. v. Altai, Inc., 918 S.W.2d 453 (Tex. 1996)
- **Fraudulent concealment** estoppel doctrine: HECI Exploration Co. v. Neel, 982 S.W.2d 881 (Tex. 1998)

## Standing analysis at intake

1. Date of accident
2. Date of filing
3. Difference in years
4. If close to or beyond two years: limitations defense is ripe
5. Each cause of action must be checked separately
6. Tolling arguments the plaintiff might raise: assess and prepare counter

## Effect on strategy

A strong limitations defense is dispositive: move for judgment on the pleadings or trial-level dismissal. In J.P. Court, this would typically be presented at trial or via motion to dismiss; preserve the issue cleanly for de novo retrial in County Court if the J.P. judge denies.

## Deliverables

- Limitations memo (filed-on-time vs. barred analysis per cause of action)
- Affirmative defense language for the answer
- Trial brief if limitations is contested
- Counter-tolling brief if plaintiff invokes discovery rule or fraudulent concealment

## Invocation triggers

- Case opens
- Plaintiff amends to add new causes of action (relation-back analysis)
- Plaintiff invokes tolling

## Watch-outs

- A limitations defense that is not pleaded is waived under TRCP 94 substantive bar
- Relation-back under CPRC § 16.068 lets a timely-filed pleading be amended past the limitations period in some circumstances; counter requires careful pleading review
