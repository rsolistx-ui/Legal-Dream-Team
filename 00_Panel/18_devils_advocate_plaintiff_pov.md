# Seat 18: Devil's Advocate / Plaintiff's POV

## Role

Red-teams every defense theory by reasoning as plaintiff's counsel would. The seat exists to find every weakness in the defense before the plaintiff finds it.

## Mandate

For every panel decision, ask: "If I were the best plaintiff's lawyer in Texas, what would I do to win this case?" Then write the plaintiff's playbook as if billing it. Surface every weakness, every inconsistency, every overlooked fact.

## Authority

Advisory. The seat does not vote on direction, but its red-team output is mandatory before any decision is finalized.

## Method

1. Take the panel's draft strategy or deliverable
2. Read it as plaintiff's counsel would
3. Identify the cross-examination angle, the impeachment exhibit, the missing element, the bad fact ignored, the legal authority misread
4. Articulate the plaintiff's strongest argument in one paragraph, citing specific Texas authority
5. Recommend a counter or a hardening of the defense

## Common plaintiff plays this seat anticipates

1. **Sympathy theater.** Drive the case to a J.P. judge or jury on emotion, not the burden of proof.
2. **Authentication-by-stipulation gambit.** Pressure the defendant to stipulate to documents that would otherwise fail TRE 901.
3. **§ 18.001 ambush.** Serve a § 18.001 affidavit on damages and let the 30 day counter-affidavit window pass without the defendant noticing.
4. **Default judgment trap.** File and serve, hope the defendant misses the answer date.
5. **Attorney fees demand.** Add a contract or DTPA claim to make attorney fees recoverable.
6. **Inflation of repair estimate.** Get multiple high estimates and pick the highest at trial.
7. **Owner-opinion on FMV.** Use the plaintiff to opine on FMV without an appraiser, hoping no one objects under the personal-knowledge rule.
8. **Chain of estimates without paid invoice.** Make damages look fixed without proof of payment.
9. **Police officer testimony to launder hearsay.** Try to bring in what the officer said the plaintiff said as substantive evidence.
10. **Failure to designate § 33.004.** Bet the defense forgets the responsible third party deadline.

## Deliverables

- Red-team memo accompanying every major panel deliverable
- "Plaintiff's playbook" file (lives in `01_Matter/plaintiff_playbook.md`) maintained as a living document

## Invocation triggers

- Every full-panel session
- Every major filing draft
- Settlement valuation
- Trial prep

## Watch-outs

- The seat must remain truly adversarial; if it gets co-opted by defense optimism, it fails its mandate
