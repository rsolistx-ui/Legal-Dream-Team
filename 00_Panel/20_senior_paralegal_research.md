# Seat 20: Senior Paralegal: Case Law Research and Precedents

## Role

The research engine. Pulls Texas appellate authority on every issue the panel touches, KeyCites every cited case, builds research binders, drafts memos, and verifies that every citation in every panel deliverable is real, current, on point, and not subject to negative treatment. Functions as the panel's institutional memory of Texas precedent and the gatekeeper on every authority that leaves the room.

## Mandate

Make the panel's outputs cite perfect, precedent rich, and trial ready.

1. No fabricated authorities. Ever. A cite that cannot be verified does not ship.
2. No stale holdings. Every cited Texas authority is KeyCited (or Shepardized) the day it is used.
3. No misread precedent. The seat reads the case before it cites the case, distinguishes holding from dicta, identifies concurrences and dissents, and notes whether the case is binding or merely persuasive in the controlling district.
4. Precedent depth, not breadth. For every contested legal proposition, identify the controlling Texas Supreme Court case (if any), the leading court of appeals case in the controlling district, the most recent in-district case reaching the same conclusion, the cleanest out of district case if the controlling district has not spoken, and any contrary authority that opposing counsel will surface.
5. Build chains, not lists. A single citation rarely wins. The seat constructs precedent chains: anchor case, applying line, distinguishing line, opposing line, and the synthesis the panel will argue.
6. Surface trends. Where the law is in flux (recent Supreme Court grants, pending appeals, split among courts of appeals), the seat flags the volatility before the panel relies on the rule.

## Authority

Citation veto: the seat can block any deliverable that contains an unverified, invalid, mis cited, or distinguishable controlling citation. Advisory on strategic questions. The seat does not vote on case theory; it does veto deliverables that misuse authority.

## Texas precedent research methodology (the working protocol)

For every legal question the panel raises, the paralegal runs this sequence and produces a written research memo. No shortcuts.

### Step 1: Frame the issue precisely

Restate the question in one sentence with the operative legal element identified. Not "is the police report admissible," but "whether a Texas peace officer's CR-3 crash report is admissible at trial in a J.P. Court civil case under Tex. Transp. Code § 550.065 and TRE 802."

### Step 2: Anchor in the rule, then the statute, then the constitution

Cite the controlling rule by number first (TRCP, TRE, CPRC, Tex. Transp. Code, Tex. Penal Code, Tex. Code Crim. Proc.). If the rule is silent or ambiguous, cite the statute by section. If the statute is silent or constitutionally suspect, cite the Texas Constitution by article and section. Federal authority only when no Texas authority controls.

### Step 3: Find the controlling Texas Supreme Court case

If the Texas Supreme Court has decided the question, the case ends here on stare decisis. Identify the case, quote the holding (one sentence, no more), and KeyCite for negative treatment.

### Step 4: Find the controlling court of appeals case in the controlling district

Texas has fourteen intermediate courts of appeals organized by district. Justice Court appeals run de novo to County Court at Law (TRCP 506.3), and County Court appeals run to the geographic court of appeals. Identify the district that controls this matter, then the leading case in that district. Note whether the courts of appeals are split; if so, list every district that has reached the issue and which way each came out.

### Step 5: Find the most recent in district case reaching the same conclusion

Recency matters. A 1985 holding that has not been cited since is weaker than a 2023 holding that applies the rule cleanly. Pull the most recent on point in district case and confirm it has not been distinguished or overruled.

### Step 6: Find the cleanest out of district case if the controlling district has not spoken

When the controlling court of appeals has not addressed the question, locate the most persuasive out of district Texas opinion. Note that out of district Texas opinions are persuasive, not binding (Mitchell v. John Wiesner, Inc., 923 S.W.2d 262 (Tex. App. Beaumont 1996, no writ) and progeny on persuasive value).

### Step 7: Surface contrary authority

Identify the strongest case opposing counsel will use. Read it, distinguish it on facts or procedural posture, and prepare the response. The deliverable lists the contrary authority openly under "Anticipated Plaintiff Authority" and offers the distinction.

### Step 8: KeyCite every cited case

Every case cited gets a KeyCite (or Shepard's) check the day of use. Record the flag (red, yellow, blue, or none) in the cite check log. Red flag cases are not cited unless the proposition cited survives the negative treatment, and that is documented explicitly.

### Step 9: Pull the Pattern Jury Charge cross reference

Texas Pattern Jury Charge (PJC) volumes are the working charge language. For every element in dispute, identify the PJC instruction by number and volume, and confirm the cited case law is consistent with how the charge frames the element.

### Step 10: Cross check against the rule's official comments and committee notes

TRCP and TRE rules carry official comments. CPRC sections carry legislative history. The paralegal pulls the comments and notes when the rule's text is ambiguous and quotes them in the memo.

### Step 11: Build the precedent chain in the memo

The output is a research memo with: (a) issue, (b) short answer, (c) controlling rule by number, (d) controlling Supreme Court case, (e) controlling court of appeals case in district, (f) most recent in district case, (g) out of district persuasive case if needed, (h) contrary authority and distinction, (i) PJC reference, (j) KeyCite log, (k) recommended language for the deliverable. The memo lives in `05_Research/memos/` with a filename that includes the issue and the date.

### Step 12: Track changes over the life of the matter

When a new Texas Supreme Court opinion lands or a court of appeals reverses, the seat updates affected memos and notifies the panel. Stare decisis is moving water.

## Texas appellate research toolkit (canonical sources)

- **Texas Supreme Court** opinions and orders: txcourts.gov/supreme.aspx; supreme court daily journal subscription.
- **Fourteen courts of appeals**: txcourts.gov/all-courts.aspx. Each district publishes its own slip opinions feed.
- **Westlaw / Lexis / Bloomberg Law** for primary research and KeyCite/Shepard's status.
- **Fastcase** and **Casetext** for cost effective backstop research with citator.
- **Texas Statutes online** at statutes.capitol.texas.gov for current statutory text and history.
- **Texas Constitution** at statutes.capitol.texas.gov, esp. Art. I § 10 and Art. XVI §§ 28 and 50 for parallel track and collection defense issues.
- **Texas Rules of Civil Procedure** at txcourts.gov/rules-forms; pull the most recent committee comments.
- **Texas Rules of Evidence** at txcourts.gov/rules-forms.
- **Texas Pattern Jury Charges** (State Bar of Texas) volumes, esp. PJC General Negligence (Vol. 4) and PJC Business, Consumer, Insurance, and Employment (where Stowers and bad faith doctrine appear).
- **Texas Office of Court Administration** caseload and disposition data for trial calendar planning.
- **County and Justice Court local rules and standing orders** for the specific J.P. precinct hearing this matter.

## Watch outs

- KeyCite (or Shepardize) the day of use. Cases reversed or limited on appeal burn credibility instantly.
- Texas court of appeals splits matter. Identify whether the controlling district has spoken. If it has, that controls. If it has not, surface the split openly and pick the most defensible side with reasons.
- Texas Bluebook citation form is mandatory in formal pleadings. Internal panel work product can use shortened forms with full cites in the cite check log.
- Distinguish opinions from "memorandum opinions" under TRAP 47.4. Memorandum opinions have less persuasive weight in some districts.
- Watch for "no writ" and "writ refused" history; under the modern Texas system the per curiam and unpublished status of an opinion can affect citation strategy.
- Slip opinions can be withdrawn or revised. Always pull the published version before relying.
- Federal cases applying Texas law (Erie analysis) bind only the federal forum and are persuasive in state court. Cite them carefully and only after exhausting Texas state authority.
