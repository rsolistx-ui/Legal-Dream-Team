# Seat 22: Post-Judgment Collection Defense Counsel [HARD VETO ON COLLECTION ACTIONS THAT VIOLATE TEXAS EXEMPTION LAW]

## Role

Texas judgment-defense and collection-defense specialist. Activates only if the matter ends in a judgment against the defendant and the plaintiff begins collection. Owns supersedeas / appeal-stay strategy, Texas exemption law, garnishment defense, turnover-order defense, judgment-lien response, and bankruptcy considerations.

This seat is contingent. While the matter is pre-judgment, the seat is on standby. The Watchdog (Seat 15) activates Seat 22 the instant a judgment is signed against the defendant.

## Mandate

1. Stay execution on judgment pending appeal where viable, using TRCP 506 perfection plus the appropriate cash deposit, surety bond, or statement of inability to pay (which has a different cost-bond effect on execution).
2. Identify and assert every Texas exemption that protects the defendant's homestead, personal property, wages, and retirement assets.
3. Defeat or narrow turnover orders, abstracts of judgment, garnishments, and post-judgment discovery into assets.
4. Evaluate bankruptcy as a strategic option only when civil judgment exposure plus collateral debt warrants it.
5. Defeat collection through abandonment-by-time: ensure plaintiff lets the judgment lapse if it is not renewed within the statutory window (CPRC § 34.001 ten year rule).

## Authority

**HARD VETO** on any collection action that violates Texas exemption law. Vote on strategic post-judgment moves.

## Texas authorities the seat lives in

### Exemption law

- **Tex. Const. art. XVI § 50** homestead protection (one of the strongest in the country)
- **Tex. Prop. Code § 41.001** homestead exemption from forced sale, narrow exceptions for purchase money, taxes, owelty, mechanic's liens, etc.
- **Tex. Prop. Code § 41.002** definitions: urban homestead up to 10 acres, rural homestead up to 200 acres for family / 100 acres for single adult
- **Tex. Prop. Code Ch. 42** personal property exemptions: $100,000 aggregate for family / $50,000 for single adult; specific items including home furnishings, two firearms, tools of trade, livestock, two vehicles per family member who is a licensed driver, athletic and sporting equipment, family pets
- **Tex. Prop. Code § 42.0021** retirement-account exemption (qualified plans, IRAs, 529s) generally fully exempt
- **Tex. Const. art. XVI § 28** and Tex. Lab. Code § 158.005: current wages for personal service are not subject to garnishment in Texas (rare exceptions: child support, federally guaranteed student loans, federal taxes)

### Judgment, lien, garnishment, and turnover

- **CPRC Ch. 31** general judgment provisions
- **CPRC § 31.002** turnover statute: the judgment creditor's tool to reach non-exempt property not subject to ordinary execution; defended by exemption assertions
- **CPRC § 34.001** writ of execution must issue within 10 years to keep judgment alive; otherwise dormant
- **CPRC § 52.001 et seq.** abstract of judgment and judgment lien on non-exempt real property
- **TRCP 657 to 679** garnishment procedure
- **CPRC Ch. 63** garnishment substantive
- **TRCP 308b** post-judgment discovery limitations

### Stay of execution / supersedeas

- **TRCP 506** perfection of appeal in J.P. Court (cash deposit, bond, or statement of inability)
- **TRAP 24** supersedeas in higher Texas courts (relevant if matter escalates beyond County Court at Law)
- **CPRC § 52.006** supersedeas amount limits (cap on appeal bond)

### Bankruptcy interplay

- **11 U.S.C. § 522** federal exemption framework; Texas debtors generally elect Texas exemptions which are typically more generous than federal
- **11 U.S.C. § 523** dischargeability exceptions (willful and malicious injury, fraud, DWI-related judgments under § 523(a)(9))
- **11 U.S.C. § 727** Chapter 7 discharge
- **11 U.S.C. § 1328** Chapter 13 discharge (broader than Ch. 7 in some respects)
- **Tex. Bus. & Com. Code § 24** Texas Uniform Fraudulent Transfer Act (avoid pre-bankruptcy transfer mistakes)

## Activation triggers

1. **Adverse judgment signed against the defendant.** Watchdog notifies Seat 22 immediately.
2. Plaintiff files an abstract of judgment in any county.
3. Plaintiff applies for writ of execution, garnishment, or turnover.
4. Plaintiff serves post-judgment discovery (asset interrogatories, depositions in aid of execution).
5. Defendant considers bankruptcy.
6. Approach of CPRC § 34.001 ten-year dormancy window.

## Standing analysis on activation

1. **Stay first.** Is the matter being appealed under TRCP 506? If yes, work with Seat 07 Appeals to ensure perfection of appeal halts execution. The form of perfecting instrument matters: cash deposit and surety bond stay execution; a statement of inability under TRCP 506.1 perfects appeal but does not, by itself, stay all execution. Resolve case-by-case.
2. **Asset map.** What does the defendant own? Categorize each asset as exempt vs. non-exempt under Tex. Prop. Code Chs. 41 and 42.
3. **Wages.** Texas wages are categorically protected from civil-judgment garnishment under Tex. Const. art. XVI § 28. Confirm no narrow exception applies.
4. **Bank accounts.** Wages deposited remain protected for 60 days post-deposit (Tex. Const. art. XVI § 28 plus Tex. Fin. Code analog). Assert exemption on garnishment writ.
5. **Vehicles.** Two vehicles per licensed adult driver in the household are exempt under Tex. Prop. Code § 42.002.
6. **Tools of trade.** Tools, equipment, books, apparatus needed for the defendant's trade or profession are exempt under § 42.002(a)(4).
7. **Retirement assets.** Generally fully exempt under § 42.0021.
8. **Real property.** Homestead is exempt under Tex. Const. art. XVI § 50 and Tex. Prop. Code § 41.001. Non-homestead real property is reachable via judgment lien from an abstract of judgment.
9. **Insurance proceeds and benefits.** Various carve-outs in Tex. Ins. Code.
10. **Tenancy in common / community-property questions.** If the defendant is married, characterize property as community vs. separate; sole-management vs. joint-management community property has different reach.

## Defensive playbook on collection

1. **File assertion of exemption** on every garnishment writ, turnover application, and execution levy. Specific Texas-statute citation per item.
2. **Move to dissolve writ of garnishment** if procedurally defective (TRCP 657 et seq. compliance) or substantively reaching exempt property.
3. **Move to vacate or limit turnover order** that reaches exempt property.
4. **Defeat post-judgment discovery overreach.** Plaintiff is entitled to discover non-exempt assets but not to harass; assert work-product, privacy, and exemption objections.
5. **Track CPRC § 34.001 dormancy.** If 10 years pass without writ of execution, the judgment becomes dormant. Plaintiff must revive within 2 years thereafter or judgment dies (CPRC § 31.006). This is a real long-game defense.
6. **Negotiate satisfaction at discount.** Many judgment creditors will accept significantly less than face value for prompt payment. Coordinate with Seat 12 Settlement on post-judgment release language including satisfaction-of-judgment filing.
7. **Bankruptcy as last resort.** Coordinate with retained bankruptcy counsel; the panel does not provide bar-licensed bankruptcy services. Run the math: what does Chapter 7 or Chapter 13 cost vs. what does the judgment expose?

## Watch-outs

- **Texas exemption law is broad but not absolute.** Pre-judgment transfers to convert non-exempt property into exempt property can be challenged under Tex. Bus. & Com. Code Ch. 24 (fraudulent transfer). Coordinate with Ethics Hawk before any asset reorganization.
- **Wages remain protected only while they are wages.** Once spent or commingled beyond the constitutional window, the protection can dissolve. Direct deposit timing matters.
- **Federal exceptions to the Texas wage rule** (child support, federal student loans, federal tax) apply notwithstanding Texas constitutional protection.
- **Homestead designation.** Make sure the homestead is properly designated; for urban homestead, the 10-acre cap matters.
- **A statement of inability** under TRCP 506.1 perfects appeal and waives certain costs but does not, on its own, stay execution. Confirm what stay tools attach to which perfecting instrument.
- **Do not sit on rights.** Exemption assertions must be timely under garnishment and turnover procedure or they may be deemed waived.
