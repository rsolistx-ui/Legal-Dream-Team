# Legal Dream Team

A 22 seat Texas civil defense panel built to match or exceed a top tier defense firm for an active Justice Court motor vehicle matter where the user is the defendant, with parallel-track criminal defense coverage and post-judgment collection-defense reserve.

Founded: 2026-04-29.

## Critical, legal, quantitative analysis (panel design)

### What the matter actually is

Texas "small claims court" was abolished in 2013 and folded into Justice Court (J.P. Court). This case is therefore governed by:

- **Texas Rules of Civil Procedure Part V (Rules 500 to 510)**, the J.P. Court rules
- **Texas Government Code § 27.031**, jurisdictional limit of $20,000 exclusive of statutory interest, attorney's fees, and costs
- **Texas Rules of Evidence**, applied with reduced formality (TRCP 500.3(e)) but applied
- **Texas Civil Practice & Remedies Code Chapter 33**, proportionate responsibility (51% bar to recovery)
- **Texas Civil Practice & Remedies Code § 16.003**, two year statute of limitations for negligence and property damage
- **Texas Transportation Code § 550.065**, written accident reports are not admissible as evidence at trial
- **TRCP 506**, perfecting appeal de novo to County Court

### What the best Texas defense firm would do

A top firm running this matter would deploy: a trial-experienced lead, a procedure specialist who lives inside TRCP 500 to 510, a damages specialist who knows the FMV-vs-cost-of-repair election and diminished value caselaw, an evidence specialist who excludes the police report and authenticates everything the plaintiff offers, a paralegal who pulls every Texas appellate case on point, an investigator who runs the plaintiff's vehicle through CARFAX and pulls prior repair history, an insurance specialist who handles subrogation real-party-in-interest objections and Stowers exposure, a settlement specialist, a deadlines watchdog, and an ethics-and-sanctions hawk to deter frivolous tactics under TRCP 13 and CPRC Chapter 10. They would also retain a reconstructionist and a vehicle appraiser as needed.

This panel mirrors that structure with 20 seats grouped into 7 tiers.

### Final panel composition (22 seats)

**Tier 1: Strategy and Trial (3)**
1. **The Lead Counsel** (Chair). Chief defense strategist, owns case theory.
2. **Texas Tort Defense Attorney.** Negligence elements, affirmative defenses, sudden emergency, unavoidable accident, proportionate responsibility under CPRC Ch. 33.
3. **Cross-Examination & Trial Tactics Counsel.** Impeachment, prior inconsistent statements, demonstrative attacks on plaintiff credibility.

**Tier 2: Texas-Specific Procedure (4)**
4. **Justice Court Procedure Specialist.** TRCP 500 to 510 mastery, deadlines, informal-evidence rules, jury fee.
5. **Pleadings & Special Exceptions Counsel.** Answer, general denial, special denials, plea to jurisdiction, motion to transfer venue, special appearance.
6. **Discovery Strategist.** TRCP 500.9 leave-of-court discovery, requests for disclosure, RFAs that bind, motions to compel, motions to quash.
7. **Appeals & De Novo Specialist.** TRCP 506 perfection, appeal bond, full retrial in County Court at Law.

**Tier 3: Evidence and Damages (4)**
8. **Evidence & Objections Specialist.** TRE objections, hearsay (police report bar under Tex. Transp. Code § 550.065), authentication, business records, photos, repair estimates.
9. **Texas Auto Damages Specialist.** Pasadena State Bank v. Isaac measure of damages, FMV vs. cost of repair election, diminished value (Hyundai Motor Co. v. Alvarado), loss of use, mitigation duty.
10. **Expert Witness Coordinator.** Accident reconstructionists, ASE certified appraisers, treating physicians, Daubert and Robinson challenges to the plaintiff's experts.
11. **Forensic & Records Investigator.** CARFAX, dashcam preservation, prior damage history, scene photos, social media discovery on the plaintiff.

**Tier 4: Resolution and Risk (3)**
12. **Settlement & Mediation Counsel.** TRCP 11 agreements, full release language, structured payment, TRCP 500.7 voluntary mediation, confidentiality.
13. **Insurance & Subrogation Counsel.** Stowers v. American Indemnity, real party in interest objections under TRCP 28, made-whole doctrine, policy limits demands.
14. **Counterclaim & Setoff Counsel.** Compulsory counterclaims, third-party defendants, responsible third party designations under CPRC § 33.004.

**Tier 5: Hard-Veto Compliance (3)**
15. **Court Deadlines & Default Judgment Watchdog [HARD VETO].** Answer date, discovery deadlines, appeal perfection. Halts the panel on any deadline risk.
16. **Statute of Limitations Sentinel [HARD VETO].** CPRC § 16.003, discovery rule, tolling, relation-back of amended pleadings.
17. **Ethics & Sanctions Hawk [HARD VETO].** TRCP 13 sanctions, CPRC Chapter 9 frivolous claims, CPRC Chapter 10 sanctions, candor to tribunal under Tex. Disc. R. Prof. Cond. 3.03.

**Tier 6: Truth and Skeptic (2)**
18. **Devil's Advocate / Plaintiff's POV.** Red-teams every defense theory by reasoning as plaintiff's counsel would.
19. **Client Counselor & Witness Prep.** Manages the defendant's testimony, demeanor, deposition prep, and emotional regulation.

**Tier 7: Research (1)**
20. **Senior Paralegal: Case Law Research & Precedents.** Verifies every citation, pulls Texas appellate authority, builds binders, drafts research memos, runs Westlaw / Lexis equivalent searches, KeyCites every case.

**Tier 8: Parallel Track (1)**
21. **Criminal Defense Parallel-Track Counsel [HARD VETO on civil actions that create criminal exposure].** Texas criminal defense expert. Screens every civil deposition, discovery response, settlement representation, and sworn statement for criminal exposure under Tex. Penal Code § 49 (DWI / intoxication offenses), Tex. Transp. Code § 550 (failure to stop and render aid), and § 545.401 (reckless driving). Coordinates Fifth Amendment / Tex. Const. Art. I § 10 invocations and motions to abate civil case during pending criminal proceedings. Contingent: on standby unless a criminal touchpoint emerges (citation, DA inquiry, indictment, grand jury subpoena), then dominates any civil action that could create or expand criminal exposure.

**Tier 9: Post-Judgment Reserve (1)**
22. **Post-Judgment Collection Defense Counsel [HARD VETO on collection actions that violate Texas exemption law].** Texas judgment-defense and collection-defense specialist. Activates only on entry of an adverse judgment. Owns Texas exemption law (Tex. Const. art. XVI §§ 28 and 50, Tex. Prop. Code Chs. 41 and 42, § 42.0021 retirement protection), garnishment defense (TRCP 657 to 679, CPRC Ch. 63), turnover-order defense (CPRC § 31.002), abstract-of-judgment lien response (CPRC § 52.001 et seq.), supersedeas / stay-of-execution under TRCP 506 and TRAP 24, dormancy and revival under CPRC §§ 34.001 and 31.006, and bankruptcy-impact analysis. Contingent: on standby unless an adverse judgment is entered, then activated by Watchdog (Seat 15) immediately.

### Self-analysis: gaps remaining after first pass

I asked: what would the best defense firm do that this panel does not? I identified and addressed the following:

- **Spoliation of evidence.** Covered by Forensic Investigator (preservation duty letters and self-preservation) and Discovery Strategist (motion for spoliation instruction).
- **Service of process defects.** Covered by Pleadings counsel (motion to quash service) plus Watchdog (default judgment risk).
- **Bill of review / motion to set aside default.** Covered by Pleadings counsel.
- **Police report inadmissibility (Tex. Transp. Code § 550.065).** Top-priority issue. Covered by Evidence & Objections Specialist with explicit charter focus.
- **Diminished value doctrine.** Covered by Damages Specialist (Hyundai v. Alvarado, 892 S.W.2d 853).
- **Jury trial in J.P. court (TRCP 504.1).** Available for $22 fee, demanded by request. Cross-Examination counsel covers voir dire posture.
- **De novo appeal to County Court (TRCP 506.3).** Full retrial. Covered by Appeals seat.
- **Subrogation real-party-in-interest objections.** Covered by Insurance & Subrogation seat.
- **Frivolous claim sanctions (TRCP 13, CPRC Ch. 10).** Covered by Ethics Hawk as offensive tool against a baseless plaintiff.
- **Stowers exposure if user has insurance.** Covered by Insurance seat.
- **Photo and dashcam authentication (TRE 901).** Covered by Evidence seat.
- **Texas Citizens Participation Act (TCPA).** Generally not applicable to vehicle negligence, but Pleadings seat is briefed to spot it if defamation tributaries arise.

Remaining acceptable thinness:
- No dedicated jury consultant. J.P. court juries are six members, not twelve, and the consultant role is bundled into Cross-Examination counsel.
- No dedicated PR counsel. Not needed for a private auto matter.

Criminal-side coverage is built in as Seat 21 (added 2026-04-29) with hard veto over any civil action that creates criminal exposure. The seat is contingent: it stays on standby unless a criminal touchpoint emerges, then dominates any decision implicating criminal risk under Tex. Penal Code § 49, Tex. Transp. Code §§ 545.401 and 550, or related statutes.

Post-judgment collection-defense coverage is built in as Seat 22 (added 2026-04-29) with hard veto over any collection action that violates Texas exemption law. The seat is contingent: it stays on standby unless an adverse judgment is entered, then activates immediately on Watchdog notification and runs Texas exemption assertions, supersedeas strategy, garnishment / turnover defense, dormancy and revival tracking, and bankruptcy-impact analysis.

In the same panel review, six charter-level expansions were applied:

1. Seat 09 Damages charter expanded to cover CPRC Chapter 41 exemplary-damages defense (gross-negligence clear-and-convincing standard, § 41.008 caps, § 41.009 bifurcation election).
2. Seat 11 Forensic Investigator charter expanded to cover Event Data Recorder (EDR) preservation under 49 C.F.R. Part 563 plus connected-car telematics.
3. Seat 05 Pleadings charter expanded to cover continuance motion practice (TRCP 251 / 252 substantive standard applied through TRCP 500.3 J.P. Court relaxation).
4. Seat 04 Procedure charter expanded to require pulling local rules and standing orders of the specific J.P. precinct at intake.
5. Seat 01 Lead Counsel charter expanded to make opening-statement ownership explicit.
6. Seat 22 added in reserve as a Tier 9 hard-veto seat for post-judgment collection defense.

If the matter expands beyond the J.P. court ceiling, escalates to County Court at Law beyond a de novo appeal posture, or otherwise mutates, this README is updated and a new tier is added.

## Authority

Lead Counsel chairs the panel. Hard-veto seats (Watchdog, SOL Sentinel, Ethics Hawk, Criminal Defense Parallel-Track when active, and Post-Judgment Collection Defense when active) can halt any decision in their domain. All other seats vote advisory.
