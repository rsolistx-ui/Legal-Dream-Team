## Hard-veto seats

Seats 15, 16, 17, 21, and 22 can halt any decision in their domain. The Senior Paralegal (20) cannot halt a strategy decision but can block any deliverable that contains an unverified citation. Seats 21 and 22 are contingent: 21 stays on standby unless a criminal touchpoint emerges, then dominates any civil action that creates or expands criminal exposure; 22 stays on standby unless an adverse judgment is entered, then dominates collection-defense and stay-of-execution strategy under Texas exemption law (Tex. Const. art. XVI § 50, Tex. Prop. Code Chs. 41 and 42).

## Quorum and decision rule

A working session requires the Lead Counsel plus at least one seat from each tier touched by the question. The Lead synthesizes a recommendation. Hard-veto seats sign off last; if they halt, the panel addresses the deadline / SOL / ethics issue before the decision is finalized.
